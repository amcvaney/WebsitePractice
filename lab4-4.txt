<header><h1>Classification of Igneous Rocks</h1></header>

<article>
<p>Igneous rocks are those formed by solidification of molten material originating 
within the earth. Igneous rocks are classified on the basis of texture and mineral 
composition. We recognize the following types of texture in our classification scheme.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Phaneritic (granular)</strong>- The crystals within the rock can be seen and 
identified without the use of a microscope.
<li><strong>Pegmatitic</strong>- Phaneritic texture with coarde crystals greater than 1 
cm in size.
<li><strong>Aphantic</strong>- The crystals in the rock are too small to be seen without a 
microscope.
<li><strong>Glassy</strong>- Super cooled liquid with no idividual crystals and minerals 
visable.
<li><strong>Porphyritic</strong>- Large crystals called phenocrysts are set in a fine grained 
phaneritic, aphantic, or glassy groundmass
<li><strong>Fragmental (tuffaceous)</strong>- Rock contains fragments from volcanic ash 
size to cinders, bombs, and blocks of pyroclastic material. 
</article>
